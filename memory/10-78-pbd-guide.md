### 1. Core PBD Concepts (Master These First)
| Concept | What It Means | How to Spot It | Trading Implication |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Auction Market Theory** | Markets are double auctions; price moves on aggression vs. passivity | Aggressive market orders create wicks / single prints | Fair value = resting orders; unfair = forced moves |
| **Value Area (VA)** | ~70% of volume traded (fair prices) | Use closing prices to define edges (no complex math) | Inside VA = fade extremes; outside = high-edge setups |
| **Unfair Prices** | Price outside the VA (imbalance) | Single prints, tails, or fast moves away from VA | High probability of reversion or continuation |
| **P Setup** | Bullish impulse up → consolidation (looks like “P”) | Impulse leg + sideways handle | Buy break-in (pullback into VA) or breakout |
| **B Setup** | Bearish mirror (impulse down → consolidation) | Mirror of P | Sell break-in or breakout |
| **D Setup** | Pure range / distribution (no clear impulse) | Bell-curve profile inside VA | Fade the extremes; only trade breakouts with volume |

**Key Shortcut**: Everything **inside** the closing-price VA = institutional fair value. Everything **outside** = exaggeration → high-probability edge.

### 2. How to Run PBD Multi-Timeframe Analysis (Step-by-Step Process)
1. **Start at the Highest Timeframe (Bias Layer)** 
 - 3-month or monthly chart 
 - Identify the dominant profile: D-setup (range), early P/B, or strong imbalance. 
 - Ask: “Is the market transitioning out of balance?” (This sets your directional bias.)

2. **Drop to Intermediate Timeframe (Structure Layer)** 
 - 1-week chart 
 - Look for the P/B/D pattern that formed the most recent impulse. 
 - Confirm buyer/seller dominance with volume expansion on the impulse leg.

3. **Zoom to Daily (Entry/Trigger Layer)** 
 - Define the developing daily VA. 
 - Check if price is trading in unfair territory (above/below VA). 
 - Look for single prints or aggressive candles confirming dominance.

4. **Drill to 4-Hour / Lower (Precision Layer)** 
 - Watch the developing 4h VA tighten. 
 - Identify the exact P/B/D resolution forming in real time. 
 - Wait for volume confirmation on break-in or breakout.

5. **Synthesize (Confluence Check)** 
 - All timeframes must align or at least not contradict. 
 - Highest TF bias + lower TF unfair price + volume dominance = high-edge setup.

### 3. How to Interpret & Trade the Setups
**Primary High-Probability Rules** 
- **P-Setup (Bullish)**: Pullback into the new VA = **buy the break-in** (buyers who drove the impulse are re-entering at better prices). 
- **Breakout Continuation**: Strong close outside VA with expanding volume = join the trend. 
- **B-Setup (Bearish)**: Mirror rules for shorts. 
- **D-Setup (Range)**: Fade the extremes (short highs, long lows) **only** until a clear impulse breaks it. 

**Invalidation & Risk Management** 
- Stop always just outside the VA or POC (Point of Control). 
- Target = opposite side of the range or back to impulse origin. 
- Risk no more than 0.5–1% of portfolio per trade (our risk-parity rule). 
- If volume does **not** confirm dominance → stand aside (PBD’s greatest edge is patience).
